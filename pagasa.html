Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical,
and Astronomical Services Administration

Speaker
Mr. Arnel G. Gonzales

Believe me or not this was the longest session during the company visit. However, most substansial session. Mr. Arnel discussed several topics not only about weather, he also shared us about how to identify level of clouds and names of clouds.

One thing I will never forget was about tornaido but here in the Philippines it has 2 terms, Ipo-ipo if it's in ocean and Buhawi if it's in land.

Aside from that, I have learned that wind conditions can affect aircraft takeoffs and Mr. Arnel discussed this, one is Headwind, this refers to the wind blowing opposite to the direction of the aircraft's takeoff roll. Headwinds have the opposite effect of tailwinds. Second Crosswind, the wind blowing perpendicular to the runway. Crosswinds can pose challenges during takeoff as they can affect the aircraft's lateral stability and require skilled handling by the pilot. Lastly, Tailwind, is an important factor to consider during aircraft takeoffs. It refers to the wind blowing in the same direction as the aircraft's takeoff roll.

If I relate the discussion to Computer Science, I believe computer science will really play an important role here just for example Weather Data Visualization, computer science contributes to the development of tools and software for visualizing weather data. PAGASA utilizes visual representations, such as maps, charts, and animations, to communicate weather information effectively to the public and decision-makers. I am very thankful that Mr. Arnel, gave his time to share to us what he knows and his expertise.
